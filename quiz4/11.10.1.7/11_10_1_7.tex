\section{Exercise 10.1}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Find the slope of the line, which makes an angle of 30 degrees with the positive direction of y-axis measures anticlockwise.\\

Let the direction vector of y-axis be,

\begin{align}
\myvec{m_1} = 
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
1
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}

and the direction vector of the line be,

\begin{align}
\myvec{m_2} = 
\begin{bmatrix}
1\\
m
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}

where m is the slope of the line.\\

The cosine of the angle between the line and the y-axis is given by,

\begin{align}
\cos (\phi) &= \frac{\myvec{m_1}^{\top} \myvec{m_2}}{ || \myvec{m_1} || || \myvec{m_2} || }\\
\myvec{m_1}^{\top} \myvec{m_2} &= 
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \hspace{0.2cm} 1
\end{bmatrix}
* 
\begin{bmatrix}
1 \\
m
\end{bmatrix}
= m\\
	\norm{\myvec{m_1}}  &= \sqrt{0^2+1^2} = 1\\
	\norm{\myvec{m_2}} &= \sqrt{1^2+m^2} = \sqrt{1+m^2}\\
	\cos (\phi) &= \frac{m}{\sqrt{1+m^2}}
\end{align}










We are given that the angle between the line and the y-axis is 30 degrees.\\

\begin{align}
\phi &= 30 \Rightarrow \cos (\phi) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\\
\frac{m}{\sqrt{1+m^2}} &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \Rightarrow 2m = \sqrt{3} * \sqrt{1+m^2}\\
4m^2 = 3(1+&m^2) \Rightarrow m^2 = 3 \Rightarrow m = \sqrt{3} \text{ or } -\sqrt{3}
\end{align}

Out of this $m=\sqrt{3}$ is the correct slope as it makes 30 degrees with the positve direction of y-axis where as $m=-\sqrt{3}$ makes 30 degrees with the negative direction of y-axis.\\
\end{enumerate}
